How to retarget website visitors with facebook ads

Facebook Advertising is the most powerful platform for marketing. It offers a variety of tools that will dramatically change the way your business advertises to your target consumers. One of the most powerful tools that Facebook Advertising has to offer is the Facebook Pixel.

The Facebook Pixel is a snippet of code that goes behind the scenes on your website and tracks how users interact with your site. It helps facebook to learn what types of people are interacting with your site and more likely to click on your ads. The Facebook Pixel also helps improve the effectiveness of an ad by tracking how facebook users interact with your website after clicking on your ad. Facebook automatically improves the effectiveness of ads by learning which users are more likely to interact with your ads and only showing your ads to those people. And all of that is made possible by the Facebook Pixel.



(Here is the tutorial in video format.)

Getting Started

To install your new Facebook Pixel, start by going to business.facebook.com and logging into your business manager account.

Click on the menu on the top left.

Highlight the 'All Tools' in the bottom left.

Click on the Pixels tab.

This is the Events Manager section of your Facebook Business Manager. This is where you will be able to see your Pixel data.

Create Your New Pixel

To create your new pixel, click on the 'Add New Data Source' button and then click 'Facebook Pixel'.

Now it is time to name your pixel. You can name the pixel whatever you want. I usually make the Pixel name the same as the domain.

Make sure you insert your domain URL into the bottom input field. This ensures that facebook gives you easier setup directions when applying your pixel code.

Once you hit continue you will be brought to three options of how to install your pixel code. Follow the directions based on what you know. You will most likely be best off emailing the instructions to a developer, or adding the code using the partner integrations.

Test Your Pixel

You want to make sure that your Pixel is properly installed on your site. An easy way to do this is to download the Pixel Helper Google Chrome Extension that will test your website for Pixel activity.

Once you have the extension installed, go to your site and refresh. You should see that the extension has found your pixel.

Inside of the Pixel in the Events Manager section of Facebook Business you should see that your Pixel is active once your pixel is properly installed.

How to use your New Facebook Pixel

So now that you have your Facebook Pixel installed you can use it to retarget your website visitors, track the efficiency of your advertising, and get an oversight of website data.

Track Website Data

The Pixel tracks all sorts of website data and gives you an interface of data to help learn about your site. You can look at things like page views, conversions, events, devices and more.

Retarget Website Visitors

With the Pixel installed on your site, you can create a custom audience and show ads to people that have viewed your website. You can create an audience of all website visitors or create audiences based on specific pages of your site.

Optimize Ads Based on Conversions

The Facebook Pixel helps track the effectiveness of your ads and automatically corrects the target audience of your ad to peole that are more likely to convert. You can create custom conversions that your pixel can track for to better cater your results to your needs.

Conclusion

Now that your new Facebook Pixel is installed it is time to start putting it to work!
